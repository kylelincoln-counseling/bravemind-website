Key Role Definitions

Clinician

A trauma-trained clinician who functions as a central facilitator of the BRAVEMIND Ukraine scenario. The clinician runs the session, acting as the “Wizard of Oz,” modulating the control panel from a computer program, and is responsible for safeguarding the patient.

Clinician (Participant)

A trauma-trained clinician who wears the VR headset (Meta Quest) experiences the scenario firsthand, providing real-time feedback on realism, pacing, and clinical usability.

Interpreter (English↔Ukrainian)

Maintains accurate, paced communication between the researcher and Ukrainian clinicians.

Researcher (Remote Facilitator)

Coordinates the session over Zoom, ensures protocol fidelity, records the session per IRB, applies de-identification, maintains change logs across scenario/script versions, and guides troubleshooting without interrupting clinical flow.

Client (Person Treated)

An individual receiving trauma-focused care. The client engages in the headset experience under clinician supervision; pacing, triggers, and safety monitoring remain clinician-controlled.

Technical Operator / VR Systems Lead (Administrator)

Ensure hardware and software stability and performance. Monitor posture, cable entanglement, thermal comfort, and the onset of cybersickness; enforce stop rules; document adverse events and recovery actions.

Integration Strategy

Workflow

The clinician operates the system and session while the client experiences it. The interpreter is engaged as needed.

Technical Integration

Hardware: Meta Quest, wired Oculus Link to a VR-ready Windows PC. Software: Meta Quest Link, BRAVEMIND Ukraine, and Zoom are configured.

Safety & Governance

Clear the physical area and set up the operator and client space. Pause and establish an abort protocol. The interpreter's role is to identify distress signs that are missed.
