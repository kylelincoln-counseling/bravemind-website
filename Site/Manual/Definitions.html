Core Roles

Clinician

A trauma-trained clinician who functions as a central facilitator of the BRAVEMIND Ukraine scenario. The clinician runs the session, modulating the control panel from a computer program, and is responsible for safeguarding the client.

Client

An individual receiving trauma-focused care. The client engages in the headset experience under clinician supervision; pacing, triggers, and safety monitoring remain clinician-controlled.

Clinical Concepts

Virtual Reality Exposure Therapy (VRET)

A therapeutic technique using virtual reality (VR) to provide sensory cues that generate a sense of presence, which can facilitate the processing of a traumatic experience. The clinician guides the client through VR scenes that approximate the trauma memory.

Eye Movement Desensitization Reprocessing (EMDR)

A structured therapy protocol adapted for use with BRAVEMIND Ukraine VR. It involves processing triggers and installing a Positive Cognition while using Bilateral Stimulation (BLS).

Subjective Units of Distress Scale (SUDS)

A self-reported rating (typically 0-10) used by the client to communicate their level of distress to the clinician at regular intervals during an exposure session.

Hot Spots

Specific moments in the trauma memory that cause more anxiety than the rest of the narrative. In VRET, the clinician may repeatedly expose the client to these moments in detail.

Positive Cognition (PC)

An adaptive or positive belief used in the EMDR protocol (e.g., "I am safe now," "I did the best I could"). The goal is to install this belief so it feels true to the client.

Bilateral Stimulation (BLS)

The use of alternating, rhythmic stimuli (e.g., tactile or auditory) to facilitate the reprocessing of traumatic memories during EMDR.

Simulation Sickness

A potential feeling of discomfort, similar to motion sickness, that can occur from prolonged use of VR. Clinicians must monitor the client for any signs of discomfort and take breaks as needed.

Application & Interface

Head-Mounted Display (HMD)

The Virtual Reality headset (e.g., Meta Quest 2) worn by the client. It displays the virtual environment and tracks the client's head movements.

Control Interface

The clinician's dashboard on the PC for managing the VR scenario in real time. From this screen, the clinician can activate triggers, adjust settings, and change the client's location.

Patient Application

The BRAVEMIND Ukraine software that runs on the PC and is displayed inside the client's HMD. The clinician's monitor mirrors what the client sees in this application.

Triggers

Events within the scenario that the clinician can activate from the Control Interface to simulate a sensory experience. Triggers are grouped into three categories:
1. Audio-Only: Sound-based cues (e.g., gunfire, explosions).
2. Audio and Visual: Combined cues (e.g., drones, helicopters). 3. Scenario: Events that may relocate the client or include dialogue.

Points of Relocation

Preset locations within the virtual environment (e.g., different parts of the combat zone) that the clinician can move the client to via the Control Interface.

Environmental Controls

Settings on the Control Interface that allow the clinician to adjust the virtual environment's Season (e.g., winter, summer), Time of Day (e.g., morning, night), and Weather (e.g., clear, rain, fog).

Setup & Connection Terms

Meta Quest Link

The software and wired connection method used to connect the Meta Quest headset to a VR-ready Windows PC.

Link Cable (USB-C 3.2)

A specific USB-C 3.2 cable required for a wired Link connection. It must support at least 5 Gbps data transfer and should be at least 3 meters (10 feet) long.

Minimum PC Specifications

PC standards determined by Meta to run programs via Quest Link. These standards are evolving, and it is recommended to use computers that exceed these minimums.
