Memory Reactivation (Phase 1 Integration)

(Optional; use with caution. No BLS.)

Rationale for Clinician: VR here functions as a recall cue during target identification (an alternative to Floatback/Affect Scan) and should be used conservatively to avoid memory contamination or premature triggering.

“I will briefly display a low-intensity virtual scene related to __________________ (repeat [C] representative recent experience). This is only to help identify past experiences; we will not process any memory at this time. Please let me know if you wish to stop.”

“Take a moment to look around. As you hold that recent experience in mind, what—if anything—feels familiar?”

“What does this setting remind you of from your life?”

“Notice the emotions you are having now and where you feel them in your body.”

“As you focus on the scene (pause) while holding the recent experience in mind (pause), allow your mind to float back to earlier times when you may have felt this way before and just notice what memory or memories come to mind… (pause). What memories are coming up?”

“If a specific memory arises, please say ‘pause’ and we will stop the scene to record it.”

Note: Record the client’s memories with brief description and age/grade below. (If arousal increases or dissociation/VR sickness is observed, discontinue VR, re-orient to the room, and continue without VR.)

Processing (Phase 4)

Intent

Keep EMDR as EMDR. VR serves as a contextual cue to access/maintain target activation; the target remains the memory network, not the headset scene. Maintain dual awareness; monitor dissociation closely because the headset obscures facial cues. Increase verbal check-ins and observe posture/voice/breathing accordingly.

Preconditions (Clinician Notes)

Client already tolerates VR; stop signal reviewed; BLS modality chosen in advance (tactile or tones preferred to avoid visual competition with the headset). EMDR materials explicitly allow changing BLS modality “as clinically indicated.”

Minimal Procedure

  1. Conduct standard Phase 3 assessment; confirm target, NC/PC, affects, body focus, baseline measures.
  2. Stabilize the VR scene at least necessary intensity that contextually matches the target (no dynamic “surprises”). Begin BLS (prefer tactile/tones).
  3. Run brief sets; after each 1–2 sets, obtain a verbal check-in for SUDs/association flow and dissociation signs. If SUDs spike or dissociation appears, remove headset and continue EMDR without VR; use standard blocked-processing strategies (e.g., interweaves) as needed.
  4. Proceed to Installation, Body Scan, and Closure per protocol with the headset off unless you have clear evidence the client stays well-regulated with it on.

Resiliency Check (Phase 8)

Placement

Use within Phase 8 after you verify completion criteria, and before moving to new targets or Future Templates.

Script

“Before we proceed, please bring up the memory we completed and notice how it feels now. Rate current disturbance 0–10.”

If SUD > 0, resume Phases 3–6 per worksheet.

“We will now use a brief virtual scene to simulate a realistic but controlled situation related to the work you have completed. This is an evaluation of how well the adaptive responses are retained, not further processing.”

“Please hold your positive cognition [state PC] and the desired feeling(s) as you orient to the scene. Tell me immediately if any disturbance arises.”

“Now engage the scene for a short interval.” (30–90 seconds; no BLS during this probe.)

“What are you noticing in thoughts, emotions, body sensations?”

“On 0–10, how disturbing is it now?”

Decision Rule (Not Spoken)

  • SUD = 0: Note retention; optionally raise difficulty one step (e.g., louder sound, closer stimulus) once, then re-check. If stable, proceed to next treatment-planning step or Future Template refinement.
  • SUD > 0: Stop VR; re-access target or present trigger and resume Phases 3–6 per worksheet until criteria met. Document as a present trigger uncovered in reevaluation.

Documentation (Clinician Notes)

Scene used, duration, peak SUDs, PC/VOC check, any dissociation/VR tolerance issues, and whether reprocessing was required.

Future Template

Note: The following protocol is adapted from the procedural steps outlined in "BRAVEMIND UKRAINE AND EMDR: UTILIZING FUTURE TEMPLATES IN POSTTRAUMATIC STRESS DISORDER THERAPY" (2025).

Introduction

“You have just completed work on a present trigger [e.g., related to the sound of explosions] and came to that positive belief of (repeat the Positive Cognition (PC)) [I can stay calm under pressure].”

“Now, we are going to use virtual reality to imagine a future scenario and develop a template. Later, we will introduce some challenging scenarios within this environment. These may include [sounds of aircraft/helicopters, drones, or witnessing an attack on a building]. What are your thoughts? How do you feel about using this approach?”

Step 1: Identify Desired Outcome

Identify a future situation (similar to the reprocessed present trigger) where a more adaptive response is needed. Ensure the client has the skills needed to implement it and address skill building as needed.

“Let's get you set up with the VR headset.”

[Give the client a few moments to familiarize themselves with the VR environment.]

“How are you feeling with the equipment on? Do you feel any motion sickness? Does everything feel comfortable? Do you feel any tension, tightness or unusual sensation? If yes, ask: Where in your body do you feel that?”

Then ask: “What is a future situation [similar to what you are encountering in this VR environment]?”

“Do the words (repeat PC from present trigger) [I can stay calm under pressure] fit?” (If not, identify their preferred PC.)

“How would you like to feel in that future situation?”

Step 2: Virtual Reality Experience of the Future Scene

Ask the client to imagine that future scene/image of the experience while holding in mind the PC and the desired feeling.

“I'd like you to imagine yourself responding effectively to this situation. With the new positive belief (repeat the PC) and a feeling of (repeat desired feeling or state), imagine stepping into this scene. Notice how you are handling the situation and what you are thinking, feeling, and experiencing in your body.”

After a sufficient pause, ask: “What are you noticing?”

If the client's response is NEGATIVE or uncertain, reprocess directly by adding sets of fast tactile and/or auditory BLS with client reports between sets until neutral. If POSITIVE: Reinforce the scene/image and strengthen with fast BLS. Install the PC until VOC is 7 (or ecologically adaptive).

Step 3: Generate a Challenging Situation within the Virtual Environment

When appropriate for the client: Create a scenario where there is a challenge and generate an adaptive response to that situation. Process and install PC to VOC of 7 for each situation.

“Now we are going to introduce some challenging situations within the VR environment. I want you to imagine yourself responding effectively to this challenge. Keep in mind the positive belief (repeat the PC) you have about yourself and the desired feeling (repeat desired feeling or state). Here comes the scenario [of hearing sirens].”

Step 4: Run a Movie (Without VR Headset)

Ask the client to run a MOVIE of the sequence from start to finish (without BLS) responding adaptively to the situation, holding in mind the PC and noticing the positive feelings and sensations.

“Now for this next part, you can please remove the VR headset. I would like you to run a movie from beginning to end dealing effectively with this situation, holding in mind the positive belief (repeat the PC) you have about yourself and noticing the positive feelings and sensations. Let me know when you get to the end of the movie or if you bump into any disturbance or difficulty.”

Once the client signals, ask: “What are you noticing?”

If NEGATIVE or uncertain, reprocess directly by adding sets of long/fast BLS with client reports between sets until neutral.

If POSITIVE: Add BLS as the client runs the movie again to strengthen the positive feelings, then ask, “What are you noticing?”

If neutral/positive, install the PC until VOC=7 (or ecologically adaptive).

Installation (Only if needed)

“Bring up the future situation and those words (repeat the selected PC). From 1 completely false to 7 completely true, how true do they feel to you now?”

“Hold the situation and the words (repeat PC) together.” (Apply BLS)

“Let it go... take a breath... What are you noticing now? (pause for response) Notice that.” (Apply BLS)

Check VOC, adding sets of BLS until the VOC no longer strengthens.

“Bring up the future situation and those words (repeat the selected PC). From 1 completely false to 7 completely true, how true do they feel to you now?”

Once the VOC=7 (or ecological) and no longer getting stronger, add another set of BLS and elicit feedback.

If Future Template Becomes Blocked

If related to hesitancy, unfamiliarity or needed skill(s), identify difficulty(ies), problem solve, teach skill(s) and/or strengthen resources. Then return to the Future Template.

If another trigger is present, reprocess with Phases 3-6, followed by Future Template.

Explore if there are any Feeder Memories or Blocking Beliefs. Reprocess using Phase 3-6. Then identify and reprocess a related Present Trigger followed by the Future Template.

Reminder: Use Closure (Phase 7) procedures before closing at the end of every session.
